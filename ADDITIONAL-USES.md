## 1. Additional Permitted Uses

The following uses are permitted under this document in addition to the
noncommercial, personal, and organizational uses already permitted by the
Base License — even if they might otherwise be considered commercial:

- **Community weather sharing.** Homeowner associations, neighborhood groups,
  voluntary community associations, and similar organizations sharing weather
  data with their members — provided the organization generates no revenue from
  the weather data or the website displaying it.

- **Family-owned farms and agricultural operations.** Family-owned or
  family-operated farms and agricultural operations with fewer than 50 employees
  that are not publicly traded on any stock exchange.

- **Amateur radio weather stations.** Weather stations operated by licensed
  amateur radio operators as part of their amateur radio activities, and citizen
  science weather networks (e.g., CWOP, Weather Underground personal stations).

- **Agricultural cooperatives and CSA programs.** Agricultural cooperatives,
  community-supported agriculture (CSA) programs, and farmer's market
  organizations sharing weather data relevant to their members and operations.

- **Tax-exempt organizations.** Organizations recognized as tax-exempt under
  United States Internal Revenue Code sections 501(c)(3), (c)(4), (c)(6), or
  (c)(7) — or the equivalent tax-exempt status under the laws of any other

## 2. Commercial Use — License Required

The following uses require a separate paid commercial license from the Developer.
If your use falls into any of these categories, you must obtain a commercial
license before deploying Clear Skies:

- **Advertising.** Displaying advertising of any kind on a Clear Skies–powered
  website, including banner ads, sponsored content, affiliate links, and
  programmatic ad placements.

- **Paid access.** Charging visitors for access to weather data displayed by
  Clear Skies, including paid subscriptions, memberships, premium tiers, or
  gated content of any kind.

- **Managed hosting.** Operating Clear Skies as a managed service, hosted
  platform, or software-as-a-service offering for third parties.

- **Revenue-generating marketing.** Using a Clear Skies–powered weather page as
  a marketing tool that directly generates revenue — for example, a resort
  weather page designed to drive bookings, a real-estate site using local
  weather data to attract buyers, or a tourism website monetizing visitor
  traffic.

- **Large organizations.** Any use by a publicly traded company or by an
  organization with more than 50 employees — except government agencies and
  tax-exempt organizations, which are permitted under the Base License and
  Section 1 above regardless of size.

- **Resale and bundling.** Reselling, white-labeling, rebranding, or bundling
  Clear Skies (or a derivative work) as part of a commercial product or service.
